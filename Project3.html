Lost Kingdoms

View Project

Overview

An ambitious, for fun Work In Progress. Lost Kingdoms has extensive systems, focused around Kingdom building. In the Guild House and Expeditions Dev Log, an expedition is shown where the player can complete a mission to earn rewards and experience. The expeditions are procedurally generated, and the missions given to the player as a random selection (think hell divers). The player can farm materials in these areas. As the player progresses the main story, new expeditions are unlocked with access to rarer materials, but more difficult enemies. The game currently features 4 enemy factions - Zombies, Boars, Skeletons, Dark Elves, and later Dragons. Expeditions involves diving into the unknown, completing the objective, and escaping with anything looted.

Technical Deep Dive

Traps/Item placement feature

The trap/item placement feature allows traps and other items to be placed in scenes. Their position is recorded, allowing traps/items.furniture to be placed safely and retain their data persistence

Object pooling and other optimisations

Due to the large number of items that may be placed at any time, the game uses object pooling system to place pre-existing objects, removing the need to create them on the fly. The system also merges large numbers of untouched numbers, similar to minecraft where their quantities are merged into a single block.

Boss Battle

A Boss battle with unique mechanics, such as using traps to stun the boss, or hiding behind pillars to make them collapse onto the boss when he charges. The boss also retreats to heal and has various moves

Ability System

The game has a expansive ability/spell casting system that allows players and party members to use different types of abilities, whether offensive, supporting or healing. Each ability has its own progression/mastery, allowing abilities to be levelled up and improved. There are multiple skill sets, allowing players to create and cycle through various abilities.

Party System

The Party System allows the player to switch and control any member of the party. Party members can follow the player, support them in combat. Party members can be downed, where they can be revived by the player and other party members. They can also revive the player and have different priorities based on their various factors such as their affinity.

Training Grounds

The training grounds acts as an arena for player to test their weapons and abilities. It also has various training missions from base defense to Night survival

Shader Showcase: Dynamic Lava + Directional aiming

Using stochastic texturing shader to add randomness to the repeating lava, the 2D shader graph also provides rotation and translation to provide dynamically moving lava in the dungeons. The video also shows the full 2D directional aiming of the Spine2D, allowing characters to aim and attack in all directions with their animated sprite.

Farming / Neutral creatures

As part of the Kingdom building, there are neutral animals which the player can farm. Cows can be milked, chickens drop eggs, and other animals can be slaughtered for their items such as leather and meat.

Vaults

By searching for clues throughout the game, the player can find rare skill books to learn unique skills. This triggers a mini game to unlock the vault from the clues gathered.

Kingdom Building + Storage + Crafting

The game features an extensive Kingdom building system. In the video, I use a hidden storage chest to get materials for the showcase. The storage is persistent, and allows retrieval and depositing of items. The game features a HQ, which starts as a tent, and as it is upgraded changes into a larger structure. In the video it starts as a small tent at level 1, and becomes a larger tent at level 2. Upgrading the HQ provides numerous benefits. Upgrading buildings provide benefits such as increased stats, healing buffs; farm buildings give item production; research buildings reduce upgrade costs; and buildings like the smithy in the video unlock new tiers of crafting recipes such as wooden and stone tools/weapons. There are also defensive buildings such as the archer towers, which may be upgraded to help with Kingdom defense missions. The Kingdom follows the day night cycle, so light crystals can be created.

Day/Night System

The Day Night system runs on a time system and affects various aspects of the game, most notably the lighting. Players can sleep to pass eight hours to skip night.

Dungeons + Harvesting

The player can give items to party members, such as harvesting tools to help to harvest many of the various resources. Harvesting tools can also be used to destroy buildings/fortifications (e.g wooden walls can be destroyed by an axe more easily). Different tools are more effective of different resource types. Currently, there are wooden, rock/stone/mineral, and ground types (e.g sand). There are different tiers of resources, which have a minimum harvesting value, encouraging players to upgrade their gear to access higher level resources, like in many other games such as minecraft. Tools also have durability.
