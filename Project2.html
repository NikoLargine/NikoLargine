My Role: Solo Developer

As a solo developer, the project was created as a fun personal project. The project focuses on Sprite characters, Spine2D playable characters, and a believable, interactable environments to enchance the player experience on their adventure. It expands on the systems in Trash Through The Dimensions, introducing more streamlined interactions, advanced inventory systems, shop systems, and more.

  • UI/UX Design & Implementation
  • Spine2D animated characters

Technical Deep Dive

Unity Lighting system

To enhance the magic of the aquarium experience, runtime states were used to track the aquarium lighting, allowing the player to control and interact with their environment.

Inventory System

Inventory system that contains consumables, key items, equippable items such as weapons, flashlights, and traps that can be placed. The inventory has data persistence and can be rearranged as the player wishes. The player may also give items to their party members.

Quest System and Quest Log

An advanced quest system that allows for any type of quest. The quest supports long quests with requirements, various steps, as well as quest rewards and world manipulation depending on their progress. In The Ghost Of Us, quests affect the environment, such as in the aquarium, fixing the power issues opens the main entry and exit to the Jellyfish Exhibit. NPCs also change their dialogue to reflect the changes in quest progression.

Runtime States

The game keeps track of runtime states. This is used to track the completion of the initial three adventures to unlock the Festival. Additionally, destructible objects remain destroyed, respawn after a timer, and allows cross scene state tracking such as whether the player has triggered the aquarium lights.

Puzzles

A puzzle minigame for the player to solve by collecting clues at the aquarium

Party System

The Party System allows the player to switch and control any member of the party. Party members can follow the player, support them in combat. Party members can be downed, where they can be revived by the player and other party members. They can also revive the player and have different priorities based on their various factors such as their affinity.

Asset Creation

An advanced dialogue system that supports sprite/Live2D characters, as well as their animations/expression changes. The dialogue system allows characters to move, SFX/music to be played, player choices, and affect run time states.

Interactable Environment

Interactable Environments for the player to engage with

Asset Creation

The creation of Sprites, illustrations, game assets, UI, and Spine2D playable characters

Shop System

The shop system allows the player to purchase items, adding a sense of immersion.

Other Features

  • Dynamic Audio: Sound effects dynamically adjust based on player proximity, creating a deeper sense of immersion.
  • 4 Distinct Environments: Each with unique adventures and challenges.
  • Equippable items: such as the flashlight and weapons which possess various parameters such as durability.
  • Interactive Minigames: Including a Haunted House and a Shooting Range.
  • NPC Management System: For characters like the mushroom folk, Alice, and Karen.
  • Core Systems: A full Save/Load system and an Ability system for special gameplay sequences.
  • Accessibility Options: Includes adjustable text speed and volume controls.
  • Advanced Ability System Details on the ability/crafting systems are further explained In Lost Kingdoms Project.
